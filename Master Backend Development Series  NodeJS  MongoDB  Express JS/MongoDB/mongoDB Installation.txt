 ### ✅ **ORM vs ODM:**  

1. **ORM (Object-Relational Mapping):**  
   - Used for **SQL databases** (e.g., MySQL, PostgreSQL).  
   - **Maps objects to relational tables**.  
   - **Examples:** Sequelize, TypeORM.  

2. **ODM (Object-Document Mapping):**  
   - Used for **NoSQL databases** (e.g., MongoDB).  
   - **Maps objects to documents**.  
   - **Examples:** Mongoose, Prisma.  

✅ **Key Difference:**  
- **ORM** → For **SQL** (table-based databases).  
- **ODM** → For **NoSQL** (document-based databases).
